What are the Causes of Water Pollution?

Water pollution has been a constant threat to our environmentâ€™s wellness. A lot of factors trigger this environmental catastrophe. This can be from sewage and water waste. The UN estimates that over 80% of wastewater worldwide returns to the environment untreated or unreused; in the least-developed nations, this percentage exceeds 95%. Another cause are large oil spills from oil drilling operations on the ocean. In addition to this, A major source of water contamination is industrial waste. Toxic chemicals and pollutants are produced by many industrial locations, and some lack adequate waste management systems. Occasionally, industrial waste is disposed of in adjacent freshwater systems. By altering the temperature of freshwater systems, the hazardous chemicals that leak from this trash can endanger marine life and render the water unfit for human consumption. Lastly, industrial waste can result in "dead zones," which are regions of water with so little oxygen that they are unsuitable for marine life.
